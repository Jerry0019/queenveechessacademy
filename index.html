Who We Are

At Queen Vee Chess Academy, we are dedicated to transforming lives through the power of chess. Founded with the belief that every child deserves a chance to thrive, we empower internally displaced children in Northern Nigeria with strategic thinking, emotional resilience, and a renewed sense of hope.

What We Do

Through our flagship program, the Chess in IDP Camps Initiative, we use chess as a tool to address trauma, enhance critical thinking, and foster belonging among children affected by displacement. Our work is grounded in the idea that chess can be more than a game—it can be a pathway to healing and growth.

Our Impact

  • Helped 50 children return to school.
  • Supported four boys from IDP camps to represent Adamawa State at the National Youth Games, with one placing 4th on Board 4.
  • Created an environment where displaced children can learn, heal, and dream beyond their current circumstances.

Our Vision

We envision a world where every child, regardless of their background, has the opportunity to grow, thrive, and redefine their narrative.

Our Mission

To empower displaced children through chess and education, fostering resilience, critical thinking, and a brighter future.

Support Us

The Gift of Chess global ambassador for Nigeria and QueenVee raise funds for our Chess in Internally Displaced Persons (IDP) camp program. Internally Displaced Children (IDP kids) are children that were forced to leave their homes due to insurgency in North East Nigeria. QueenVee Chess Academy Yola, created in 2021, is an organization using the game of chess to help these children through situations of uncertainty, to give them access to quality education, to occupy their minds in a positive way, and to give them access to opportunities in life. We have about 7 IDP camps in Adamawa State alone, with over 500 displaced kids in each of these camps, and we have IDP camps in almost all the 19 states in Northern Nigeria. We are currently trying to reach other young chess players outside their IDP camps and also to give them quality education through scholarships. We have been able to enroll 11 of them into secondary school.
